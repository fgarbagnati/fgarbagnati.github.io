So often, people fall into patterns and ongoing challenges within their primary relationship or within their family. Many times, these patterns result in conflicts, disconnections, hurt feelings, disappointments, and a general sense of alienation and loneliness; and on top of this, folks begin to feel stuck, and even hopeless that things can get better.

COUPLES

In couples, both sides can begin to feel that they are misunderstood, or that they are “right”. Each person begins to fall back on familiar-but-ineffective communication styles and conflict resolution styles. This leads to diminished intimacy and closeness, high levels of conflict, various inhibitions, and, sometimes, infidelity. In the end, I find that this can lead to questions about whether the relationship is even viable anymore. A lot of folks in these circumstances choose to do couples therapy, in lieu of one-on-one therapy, as a means of examining and exploring relationship patterns and dynamics.

I help couples:

  • Hone and clarify the lenses through which they see each other
  • Re-connect with feelings of love, tenderness, and closeness
  • Develop, Improve, and Deepen communication and listening
  • Explore relational values to clarify expectations and needs for each other, and from each other.

FAMILIES

In families, members can find themselves mired in confusing and muddied relationships; sometimes families can get stuck on old patterns that no longer work as the family evolves and the children grow. Inevitably, conflict, disconnection, and even loneliness can result. I find that this can lead to: children “acting out”; parents either overly-asserting control, or throwing their hands up and not knowing what to do; children not trusting parents, and parents not trusting children. A lot of folks in these circumstances choose to do family therapy in lieu of one-on-one therapy, as a means of examining and exploring intra-family dynamics.

I help families:

  • Understand the family system in such a way that each person’s behavior makes sense within a context
  • Clarify and update roles and expectations, particularly as children transition to adolescence and young adulthood
  • Create agreements whereby each person understands what is expected from them and from others
  • Understand and share power in ways make sense to everyone and feel equitable to everyone
