Welcome to my therapy website, and thank you for checking it out. Coming to therapy can be daunting, or intimidating, or shame-inducing, and for many folks, therapy isn’t something they thought they ever would’ve considered. In this light, I respect your decision to even explore the possibility of therapy.

I have worked for almost 25 years in the field of psychotherapy and community-based mental health, counseling folks who have struggled with a range of internal, interpersonal, relational, and environmental challenges and questions. I’ve spent many years working with folks who experience intense internal experiences; this work has helped me develop a sensitivity to, and a comfort with, the myriad of ways that people express their pain and their desires.

In these trying times that we live in, these questions take on extra importance, as people search for meaning, connection, relationship, success, happiness, and a lens through which to make sense of things. I help my clients to do this through a variety of reflective approaches: humanistic, relational, cognitive, psychodynamic, and existential.
